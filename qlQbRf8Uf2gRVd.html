Growth-supporting vitamins

Growth-supporting vitamins

In addition to the genetic predisposition that we get from our parents, proper nutrition and sufficient vitamins can support normal growth. To achieve maximum growth, you need monitor bone health and provide the body with the best nutrients.

The most powerful effect on growth is provided by the following vitamins.

Vitamin D.

This is the main vitamin responsible for bone strength and growth. Its lack in infancy and childhood can lead to growth retardation and development of children. If a person does not manage to synthesize this vitamin as much as the body needs, his bones and teeth become brittle and brittle. To get enough vitamin D, you need to spend at least half an hour in the sun a day. In addition, you can eat foods rich in this vitamin, such as fish oil, liver, milk, eggs, mushrooms, citrus fruits.

Vitamin B1.

This vitamin also promotes growth. In addition to its benefits for growth, this vitamin helps digestion, protects the heart and nervous system. Some foods that you can get vitamin B1 from are brewer's yeast, wheat and rice bran, and nuts, pork.

Vitamin B2.

This vitamin, also known as Riboflavin, is extremely important for growth. It supports the growth of bones, nails and hair. It is also called a growth factor, because the body needs it mainly for protein synthesis. Foods rich in vitamin B2 are eggs, fish, milk, and greens vegetables.

Vitamin C.

This vitamin is also called ascorbic acid and helps maintain the strength of bones and teeth through regular intake. Vitamin C has antioxidant properties and reduces the risk of diseases, as well as promotes increased growth. Foods rich in vitamin C are peppers, tomatoes, kiwis, broccoli, citrus fruits and berries.

Vitamin A.

Some of us know this vitamin as retinol. It is involved in the process of bone growth and stimulates physiological growth, as it contributes to the formation of new cells in the body. You can get vitamin A from dairy products, eggs, beef, salmon, carrots, tomatoes, peppers and leafy green vegetables.
