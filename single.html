Narcissistic personality disorder

Narcissistic personality disorder is a mental abnormality in which a person's sense of self-importance (ESH) and significance increases. Narcissists need constant recognition from the outside they do not feel empathy for other people. Behind the mask of immense confidence of these patients lies an easily vulnerable self-esteem, which is extremely sensitive to even the slightest criticism.

Narcissism as a personality disorder leads to problems in many areas of a person's life, for example, in their relationships with others. relatives and friends, at work, at school. Often, patients suffering from this disorder become unhappy and very upset when they are not given enough attention and little praise.

What are the causes of narcissism?

Scientists have not yet determined the exact cause of the disease. As in the case of with other mental disorders, the cause is complex and complex. There is a theory that narcissism is an instinctive form of realization of the dominant instinct.

Symptoms.

Narcissism belongs to the group of personality disorders. Personality disorders are conditions in which patients behave differently they feel inadequate within the social community, which reduces their ability to lead a full life and communicate well with other people. Researchers have identified several characteristics of patients with narcissistic personality disorder. Some of them are:

- overstated emergency number;

- exaggeration of their own success and talents';

- the need for continuous recognition of achievements, even minimal and insignificant;

- expecting special treatment and receiving any benefit, regardless of whether they deserve it or not;

- inability to recognize the needs and feelings of others;

- feeling of envy for success another person;

"arrogant behavior.

With narcissism, patients go beyond the boundaries of healthy self-esteem (self-esteem) and go to extremes associated with ESV (not caring about other people and extracting only their own benefit).

How it is placed diagnosis?

The diagnosis is made on the basis of:

- characteristic signs and symptoms of the patient;

- a thorough psychological examination and interview of the patient with a specialist psychiatrist;

- physical examination, which excludes another cause of these symptoms.

Treatment narcissism's.

Treatment focuses on psychotherapy and a conversation with a psychiatrist. During these conversations, patients can learn to communicate better with other people and enjoy communication without requiring constant recognition. In addition, they can understand where their desire to rise above others and always be present is rooted best.

Because personality traits are difficult to correct, patients may need years of psychotherapy to deal with their narcissism.

There are no AIDS or medications that can be used to treat narcissism.
