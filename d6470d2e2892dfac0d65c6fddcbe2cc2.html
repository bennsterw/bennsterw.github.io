Gout: which foods can you eat and which can't?

Gout occurs also due to the consumption of certain foods or beverages. The cause is ingredients called purines, which the body metabolizes to uric acid acids (MK).

If MK is not removed from the kidneys or it is in such quantities that the body cannot cope with it, this leads to the crystallization of acid into urate monosodium. The accumulation of crystals in the joints is accompanied by severe pain, which usually begins at night and is localized mainly in the large toe.

Proper nutrition and avoiding foods that can cause a gout attack are at the heart of fighting gout. This is why doctors usually inform patients in detail about how to eat properly and maintain their health.

What foods can I eat?

People with gout it is recommended to reduce protein intake and eat mainly lean meat, fish and chicken. Pork, lamb and beef are associated with an increased risk of gout attacks, so caution should be taken when eating this type of meat. Patients with gout are not forbidden to eat dairy products, if they do not contain a large amount of fat.

Complex carbohydrates are recommended, as they are more slowly metabolized by the body, which reduces appetite, and in addition, these carbohydrates help maintain a healthy body weight.

Eat as many different fruits and vegetables as possible. They are rich in fiber, which helps to process MK and useful for health.

It is important to drink enough water throughout the day, as studies show that increased water intake may be associated with a reduced risk of gout exacerbating.

What foods are not allowed to eat?

Do not replace water with carbonated drinks with high alcohol content sugar content. These drinks are sweetened with fructose syrup, which stimulates the production of MK. Studies show that people who consume this drink are at an increased risk of developing gout.

Some vegetables, such as asparagus and spinach, can cause a gout attack, but are not as dangerous as red meat. Purines contained in vegetables are more easily excreted from the body. Experts recommend eating these vegetables raw.

Legumes contain a significant amount of purines, which makes them unsuitable for patients with gout. If you can't completely give up these foods, you should consume them in the morning minimum number.

Unpleasant news for beer lovers. Beer is a very bad choice for gout patients, and it is even more harmful to them than red meat. It increases the amount of MK in the body and makes it difficult to remove it. Wine can have the same effect, although not as strong. Therefore, specialists they recommend that their patients completely avoid alcohol consumption in order to protect themselves from attacks of gout.
