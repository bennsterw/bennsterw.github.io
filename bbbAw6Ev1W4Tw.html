Smoking as a risk factor for damage to the body

Smoking is the most important cause of premature death from a number of chronic non-communicable diseases. It has been proved that 80-90% of chronic respiratory pathologies; 80-85% of cases lung cancer( RL); 30% of all malignancies and 25-43% of CHD are directly associated with Smoking. Tobacco is an uncompromising "destroyer" of human health, which has a harmful effect on all body systems.

Cancer.

Heavy smokers, as well as passive smokers, are most at risk risk of developing diseases. There is a close relationship between the number of cigarettes smoked, the duration of Smoking and the risk of developing RL. The risk of RL when Smoking for more than 10 years and more than 10 cigarettes a day is ten times higher than in non-smokers. Ten years after quitting Smoking, the risk for former smokers is equal to the risk for nonsmoker.

The risk of cancer is noticeably higher when carcinogenic substances from the professional environment - long-term work with fibrous asbestos dust, arsenic and arsenic compounds, Nickel, chromium, hydrocarbons, radioactive substances, etc.are combined with Smoking.

Nervous system.

It is very sensitive to the effects of tobacco poisons. Nicotine (the substance which creates a strong psychological dependence) nervous system damage in two main ways: direct toxic effects on nerve cells and blood vessels spasm, which affects blood flow to the brain that it is manifested by headaches, dizziness, weight loss, decreased productivity, etc. Long-term Smoking can also affect feelings: hearing decreases and vision worsens, which increases the risk of cataracts.

Cardiovascular system.

About 25% of all diseases the cardiovascular system is associated with Smoking. This reduces the average life expectancy of a smoker by about 10 years.

Each cigarette you smoke accelerates your heart rate by about 20 beats per minute. Smoking 20 cigarettes a day is equivalent to the load you get for 8 hours of Cycling against the wind. So thus, the smoker's heart "gets tired" and" ages " faster. In addition, Smoking increases blood pressure by 20-40% due to an increase in heart rate and narrowing of blood vessels.

Smoking accelerates the formation of atherosclerotic plaques in the blood vessels, which makes it one of the most important factors the risk of CHD and cerebrovascular diseases. Burger's disease is also characteristic of smokers, in whom painful narrowing of the arteries of the lower extremities leads to necrosis and amputation.

Respiratory system.

Among the most severely affected systems from tobacco is the respiratory system. Inhaled cigarette smoke smoke irritates the respiratory tract and causes coughing and shortness of breath. However, these are only external manifestations. Along with nicotine, many other toxins enter the lungs, which cause: irritation and spasm of the smooth muscles of the bronchi; an increase in their mucous secretion and damage to the ciliated epithelium in the mucous membrane. Thus, impaired bronchial patency and normal mechanisms for clearing the bronchial tree. Acute and then chronic inflammatory diseases are formed, which disrupts the function of the lungs.

Smokers have an increased incidence of bronchial asthma, RL, and even pulmonary tuberculosis due to reduced resistance resistant to infectious agents.
